Issue 37

THE SIGNAL SPREADS

Signal Spread

Small corrections appearing across different places at once, too consistent to be random anymore.

At first, it was subtle.

A hesitation that didn’t turn into a mistake.

A moment where someone chose differently.

Then another.

And another.

Across different timelines.

Different places.

Small corrections.

Gemstone felt it immediately.

Not through sight.

Through alignment.

The field wasn’t holding because of him alone anymore.

Issue 38

THE FIRST OTHERS

The ship shifted perspective.

Focused in.

One individual.

Then another.

Then more.

They weren’t connected.

Different lives.

Different paths.

But similar traits.

They paused before reacting.

They questioned instinct.

They resisted pressure.

And when they did—

the Interloper couldn’t feed.

Gemstone watched one closely.

A decision point.

The pressure came in.

Heavy.

Trying to bend it.

The person stopped.

Breathed.

Chose differently.

The timeline held.

For the first time—

Gemstone wasn’t alone in that action.

Issue 39

THE SYSTEM RESPONDS

The grid didn’t stay passive.

It adapted.

Faster than before.

Scanning more aggressively.

Tightening its reach.

Moments that used to carry light pressure—

now carried weight.

Emotional spikes.

Fear.

Impulse.

Urgency.

Amplified.

Pushed harder.

The Interloper wasn’t losing.

It was recalibrating.

Gemstone felt it immediately.

“They’re adjusting…”

“Yes.”

“They’ve identified variance.”

That meant one thing.

This wasn’t hidden anymore.

The timelines began forming nodes.

Points where multiple outcomes intersected.

High pressure zones.

Critical decisions.

Moments where:

The Interloper concentrated there.

So did Gemstone.

So did the others.

the outcome wasn’t controlled by one force.

Issue 42

THE NETWORK WAR

The ship expanded its reach.

Not just movement anymore.

Connection.

Gemstone didn’t speak to the others.

Not directly.

But something passed between them.

Recognition.

Awareness.

Alignment.

They weren’t organized.

But they weren’t isolated anymore.

The Interloper pushed harder.

More distortion.

More pressure.

But every time it tried to bend a moment—

someone held.

And when they did—

it weakened.

Slightly.

But consistently.

Issue 43

THE SHIFT

The system began changing.

Not collapsing.

Rebalancing.

For the first time—

the Interloper didn’t control the flow.

It reacted to it.

That was new.

That meant something deeper.

It wasn’t designed to handle resistance at scale.

Gemstone stood in the center of it all.

Watching it unfold.

Not forcing anything.

Not controlling outcomes.

Just holding position.

And letting alignment spread.

Issue 44

THE THRESHOLD

The timelines stabilized—

not completely.

But enough.

The field held longer.

Moments stretched further before collapsing.

The Interloper was still there.

Still feeding.

But not dominating.

Gemstone looked forward.

There was a point ahead.

Different from everything else.

Not a node.

Not a fracture.

A threshold.

Beyond it—

the system changed completely.

The Oracle dimmed slightly.

“You’ve reached it.”

Gemstone didn’t look back.

“What happens next?”

Silence.

Then—

“Everything becomes visible.”
