Issue 22

THE FEED

Image Placeholder

Dense Field

The ship slowing in front of a field so thick with bent energy it can barely move cleanly through it.

The ship slowed.

Not because Gemstone told it to.

Because something ahead was dense.

Not matter.

Not space.

Energy.

Heavy.

He stepped forward, letting the field open around him.

The timelines here weren’t broken.

They were strained.

Bent at key moments.

He watched one unfold.

A man stood at a crossroads.

Two choices.

One simple.

One destructive.

The destructive one felt louder.

Heavier.

The man hesitated—

then chose it.

The timeline darkened instantly.

Something moved through it.

Not visible at first.

Then clearer.

It didn’t attack.

It absorbed.

The moment.

The regret.

The collapse that followed.

Gemstone’s jaw tightened.

“It’s feeding…”

The Oracle didn’t correct him.

Issue 23

THE TASTE

More timelines opened.

Different people.

Different lives.

Same pattern.

A decision point.

A moment of pressure.

And something there—

just before the choice.

Not forcing.

Leaning.

Amplifying doubt.

Amplifying fear.

Amplifying impulse.

Pushing just enough—

for the worse option to feel right.

Then feeding when it landed.

Gemstone stepped closer.

“You enjoy it.”

Not a question.

The presence reacted.

For the first time—

it acknowledged him.

Not with words.

With attention.

The timelines around him warped slightly.

Focused.

Like he had stepped into its line of sight.

Issue 24

THE BLOODLINES

The ship shifted perspective.

Zooming out.

Patterns formed at scale now.

Not individuals.

Families.

Lines stretching through generations.

Certain ones pulsed brighter.

Stronger.

More stable.

Less reactive.

Harder to bend.

The Interloper moved toward those.

More frequently.

More aggressively.

Gemstone watched it press against one.

Small disruptions.

Bad timing.

Loss.

Miscommunication.

Moments stacked over years.

Trying to fracture the line.

“They’re targeted…”

This wasn’t random.

It sought out:

strength

clarity

direction

And tried to break it.

Issue 25

THE CORRUPTION

One timeline held longer than the rest.

A family line.

Strong.

Grounded.

But not untouched.

The Interloper didn’t rush it.

It stayed close.

Patient.

Waiting for cracks.

When they came—

it moved in.

Not to destroy everything at once.

To distort.

Turn small mistakes into patterns.

Patterns into identity.

Identity into collapse.

Gemstone stepped forward.

“This is what you do…”

Not chaos.

Corruption.

Slow.

Precise.

Issue 26

THE REFUSAL

Image Placeholder

Held Line

A timeline finally refusing the push because the pressure has been quieted just enough.

He focused on one moment.

A decision about to be made.

He didn’t stop it.

Didn’t force it.

He changed the field.

Quieted the pressure.

Removed the push.

Just enough.

The person chose differently.

The timeline didn’t darken.

It held.

For the first time—

the Interloper reacted sharply.

Not calm.

Disrupted.

It wasn’t used to resistance.

Gemstone stepped back.

“That’s it…”

Not fighting it directly.

Removing what it feeds on.

Issue 27

THE HUNT

The ship pulsed harder.

More signals lighting up.

More points of pressure.

More moments about to bend.

The scale became clear.

This wasn’t one place.

It was everywhere.

Constant.

Ongoing.

A system feeding on instability across the entire field.

Gemstone looked out across it.

No anger.

No panic.

Just clarity.

“This isn’t ending.”

Not by one action.

Not by one fight.

Issue 28

THE POSITION

He stepped back into the center of the ship.

Aligned fully now.

Not reacting.

Not chasing.

Positioning.

He wasn’t there to destroy it.

He was there to:

Interrupt it.

Starve it.

Outlast it.

Across timelines.

Across moments.

Across decisions.

The ship responded.

Locking into a new pattern.

Not attack.

Coverage.

Movement.

Presence where it mattered.

Gemstone looked forward.

The field stretched infinitely.

Still unstable.

Still active.

But no longer unseen.

And that changed everything.
